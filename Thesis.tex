\begin{abstracts}
The double differential cross-section for $Z$ bosons produced in electron channel was measured in binning in rapidity and di-electron mass. For this measurement, the data was used that was collected at ATLAS detector at LHC during the 2011 at center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s} = 7$~TeV. The analysis done in this paper was focused on the central-forward $Z$ bosons, i.e. the bosons that decayed into two electrons, one of which was reconstructed of in the central region of the detector  ($|\eta| < 2.5$) and the other in the forward region of the detector $|\eta| > 2.5$). Several sources of the systematic uncertainties were taken into account, including the electron reconstruction and identification efficiencies, pileup effect, PDF sets, and differences between various Monte-Carlo simulations. The main sources of errors were found to be the efficiency of the forward electron identification and the forward electron energy resolution. The results of the analysis are in agreement with other channels of $Z$ boson production and with theoretical predictions.

The fast simulation method called Frozen Showers was developed to speed up the simulation of the Monte-Carlo data samples. The method increases the simulation speed by $\sim$25\% while introduces the errors of less than 1\% to the energy scale and resolution of the reconstructed particles. The method was used to speed up the production of all the Monte-Carlo samples used in this analysis.
\end{abstracts}
